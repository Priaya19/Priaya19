Today, I learnt on why BitLocker issues happened and how to resolve it. BitLocker issues can occur during various scenarios, including the initial encryption or decryption of drives, system updates, significant hardware changes, forgotten or lost keys, malware interference, disk errors, and alterations in Group Policy or configurations. To resolve BitLocker issues, users should first attempt to use their recovery key to unlock the drive or access their encrypted data. If the recovery key is unavailable or the issue persists, they may need to access BitLocker recovery options through the Control Panel or Command Prompt to unlock the drive or reset BitLocker configurations. For more complex issues, involving IT support or referring to Microsoft's official documentation and forums for guidance is recommended to ensure data recovery and system integrity. Regular backups are essential to mitigate potential data loss during BitLocker-related problems. To resolve BitLocker-related issues, started by using the first 8 characters of the recovery key ID and entered them into the Microsoft BitLocker Administration. After submitting this information, the system generated an unlock key. This unlock key was crucial for regaining access to the encrypted drive. Once obtained the unlock key, follow the necessary steps to unlock BitLocker, allowing to access your encrypted data and resolve the BitLocker issue. Moreover, I continued with the course titled "Power Automate - Complete Guide to Microsoft Power Automate", by learned how to create flows from scratch, starting with the basics of setting up triggers and actions.
